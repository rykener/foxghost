Here's some resources to help with furthering your practice.

## Practice Opportunities

There are many great ways to plug into Dharma practice both in the Bay Area and online! There are many more opportunities available than what is listed below.

| Sangha | Info | Website | 
|--------|------|---------|
| East Bay Meditation Center (EBMC) | Non-denominational Buddhist practice in Oakland. Strong focus on diversity and inclusion. Many different programs. | [eastbaymeditation.org](https://eastbaymeditation.org/) |
| EBMC Trans Deep Refuge Group | Zoom meetings every fourth Monday at 6:30p | [meetup page](https://www.meetup.com/trans-genderexpansive-mindfulness-deeprefugegroup/) | 
| EBMC Alphabet Sangha (Queer Group) | Meets both in person and online | [alphabet.eastbaymeditation.org](https://alphabet.eastbaymeditation.org/) |
| EBMC other groups | Disability, Parents, POC Yoga, POC Sangha | [eastbaymeditation.org/sanghas](https://eastbaymeditation.org/sanghas/) |
| San Francisco Zen Center (SFZC) | Zen Buddhist temples in San Francisco, Marin County, and the Ventana Wilderness. The sponsor organization for Trans Sangha. Lots of ways to plug in. Public programs every Wednesday evening plus every morning except Sunday | [sfzc.org](https://sfzc.org) |
| SFZC Queer Dharma | Online group that meets monthly on either the 1st or 2nd Saturday at 1pm | [Queer Dharma website](https://www.sfzc.org/practice-centers/city-center/zen-meditation-practice-city-center/practice-groups-city-center/queer-dharma) |
| SFZC other programs | Saturday practice group, Recovery, "Young" people, Zen en Español | [Practice Groups](https://www.sfzc.org/locations/city-center/zen-meditation-practice-city-center/practice-groups) | 
| Insight Meditation Center | Insight style meditation center offering daily online meditation and talks at 7am on weekdays | [insightmeditationcenter.org](https://insightmeditationcenter.org) |
| Insight Meditation LGBTQIA+ Sangha | Meets online 1st and 3rd Sundays at 5p | [insightmeditationcenter.org/lgbtqueer-sangha](https://www.insightmeditationcenter.org/lgbtqueer-sangha/) |
| Spirit Rock | Offers a variety of Vipassana programs, workshops, and retreats. | [spiritrock.org](https://www.spiritrock.org) |
| Spirit Rock Rainbow Sangha | Meets every Wednesday at 6p | [spiritrock.org/programs/drop-ins](https://www.spiritrock.org/programs/drop-ins)
| Spirit Rock other groups | Monday night, Wednesday night, BIPOC, women, recovery | [spiritrock.org/programs/drop-ins](https://www.spiritrock.org/programs/drop-ins)

## Retreat Opportunities

The heart of Buddhist practice takes place at retreat. Zen Buddhists call retreat "sesshin," which is translated as "touching the heart-mind." The Bay Area is one of the best places in the country for Buddhist retreat and the options available to you far exceed what is listed below. 

| Sangha | Info | Website | 
|--------|------|---------|
| Trans Sangha | We offer half day retreats multiple times a year | |
| SFZC | SFZC offers regular day long and multi day retreats in the Zen tradition. There are also opportunitites for short term (1-4 weeks) and long term (4+ weeks) residencies in all three of their temples. Kei or Eleanor can give you more info. | [sfzc.org](https://sfzc.org) |
| Insight Retreat Center (IRC) | IRC provides retreats both in person (Santa Cruz) and online multiple times a month in the Insight tradition. Spaces in retreats fill up fast so it's useful to register early. Marion can give you more info. | [insightretreatcenter.org](https://www.insightretreatcenter.org) |
| Spirit Rock | A Vipassana retreat center that offers retreat both in person (Marin) and online multiple times a month. Spaces on retreats fill up fast so it's useful to register early. | [spiritrock.org](https://www.spiritrock.org) |
| Deer Park | A monastery in Thich Nhat Hanh's lineage located near San Diego. | [deerparkmonastery.org/join-a-retreat/](https://deerparkmonastery.org/join-a-retreat/) |

  * About building an inclusive community, much of which focuses on Larry's experience of helping found the East Bay Meditation Center (EBMC), a multicultural sangha in Oakland. 
  * [Purchase at SFZC](https://store.sfzc.org/product/awakening-together-the-spiritual-practice-of-inclusivity-and-community-yang-larry-/773) - [SF library](https://sfpl.bibliocommons.com/v2/record/S93C3448153)

* **Zen Mind, Beginner's Mind** *by Shunryu Suzuki Roshi*:
  * This book is historically one of the most important entry gates to Buddhist practice in English-speaking Zen. It is a series of transcribed talks given by the founder of the San Francisco Zen Center.
  * [Purchase at SFZC](https://store.sfzc.org/product/zen-mind-beginner-s-mind-50th-anniversary-edition-shunryu-suzuki-/762?cp=true&sa=false&sbp=false&q=true) - [SF library](https://sfpl.bibliocommons.com/v2/record/S93C2779239)
* **Introduction to Zen Koans** *by James Ford*:
  * A wonderful and practical primer on not just the Zen practice of koans but of Zen in general. Highly recommended if you’re interested in learning more about Zen, not just the “spirit” of Zen.
  * [Purchase at SFZC](https://store.sfzc.org/product/introduction-to-zen-koans-learning-the-language-of-dragons-james-ishmael-ford-foreward-by-joan-halifax-/1383) - *unavailable at SFPL*
* **Transcending: Trans Buddhist Voices edited** *by Kevin Manders and Elizabeth Marston*:
  * A collection of essays and reflections written by transgender Buddhist practitioners of varying experiences. While the trans population has been severely (but not entirely) barred from entry into becoming authorized Buddhist teachers, it is interesting to read about how the practice has still impacted our lives.
  * [Purchase at SFZC](https://store.sfzc.org/product/transcending-trans-buddhist-voices-edited-by-kevin-manders-and-elizabeth-marston-/1119) - [SF library](https://sfpl.bibliocommons.com/v2/record/S93C3888441)
* **Turning Suffering Inside Out** *by Darlene Cohen*:
  * The late Zen Master Darlene Cohen suffered from severe chronic pain and offered extremely down-to-earth teachings about practicing with it. But this book goes beyond dealing with physical pain and presents a radical approach to being human, one that is ultimately punk as hell. 
  * [Purchase at Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/Turning-Suffering-Inside-Darlene-Cohen/dp/1570628173) - [SF library](https://sfpl.bibliocommons.com/v2/record/S93C1813064)
* **The Mountain Poems of Stonehouse** *translated by Red Pine*:
  * Stonehouse was a 14th century Chinese Zen master. His brief poems are deep and cut to the heart of his mountain hermit practice.
